First Aid

First aid is the immediate help given to someone who is injured or suddenly ill before the arrival of professional medical help.

NOTE: First Aid is not a full medical treatment. It is an emergency support you give within minutes to increase the chances of survival and reduce complications

AIMS:

Preserves life

Prevents the condition
from getting worse

Promotes recovery

Safety Tips

  • Always wear gloves before touching blood or body fluids.


  • Never give an unconscious person food or water.


  • Do not apply ice directly to skin.


  • Never break blisters.


  • Don't try to “fix” broken bones — just immobilize.


  • Always monitor the person until help arrives.

When to call the Emergency Service immediately

  • No breathing


  • Severe bleeding


  • Chest pain


  • Stroke symptoms
  • Seizures lasting more than 5 minutes


  • Unresponsiveness


  • Poisoning


  • Major burns

If Possible...


Record:

  • What happened

  • Time of the incident

  • First Aid given

  • The changes in condition

This would be useful for hospitals or a medical professional
