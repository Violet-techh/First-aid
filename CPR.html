First Aid & Emergencies

CPR

Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation

What is CPR?

CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation) is an emergency lifesaving procedure performed when someone’s breathing or heartbeat stops.

When to Start CPR

  • The person is unresponsive
  • Not breathing or only gasping
  • No signs of life

If unsure, start CPR. Doing something is better than doing nothing.

Adult CPR - Step-by-Step

1. Check Safety: Make sure the area is safe for both you and the victim.

2. Check Responsiveness: Tap their shoulder and shout “Are you okay?”

3. Call Emergency Services.

4. Check Breathing: No more than 10 seconds. Gasping is NOT normal breathing.

Chest Compressions

  • Place hands in the center of the chest
  • Push hard and fast
  • Rate: 100-120 per minute
  • Depth: 2 inches (5 cm)
  • Allow chest to fully rise each time

Rescue Breaths (If trained)

  • Head tilt and chin lift
  • Pinch nose, give 1 breath (1 second)
  • Watch for chest rise
  • Give 2 breaths then resume compressions

Continue CPR until help arrives, person breathes, or you cannot continue.

Child CPR (1-12 Years)

  • Check responsiveness
  • Call emergency services
  • Compressions: about 2 inches (5 cm)
  • Use one hand if child is small
  • 30 compressions + 2 breaths (if trained)
  • Hands-only compressions still help

Infant CPR (Under 1 Year)

  • Tap foot and call their name
  • Check breathing
  • Use 2 fingers in the center of the chest
  • Press 1.5 inches (4 cm)
  • 30 compressions + 2 gentle breaths
  • Cover baby's mouth and nose with your mouth
  • Keep the head in a neutral position

Hands-Only CPR

  • Call emergency services
  • Push hard and fast in the center of the chest
  • Don't stop until help arrives

Safety & Legal Info

Safety Rules

  • Ensure the scene is safe
  • Don't put yourself at risk
  • If breathing normally, do NOT start CPR

Disclaimer

This website provides educational information only and does not replace certified CPR training.

Myths & Facts

Myth: CPR always works.
Fact: CPR keeps someone alive until help arrives.

Myth: You can get in trouble for helping.
Fact: Good Samaritan laws protect helpers.

FAQ

What if I do CPR wrong? Any effort is better than none.

Do I need mouth-to-mouth? Hands-only CPR is effective for adults.

What if they have a pulse? If not breathing normally → start CPR.
