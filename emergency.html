Quick Emergency
Help Guide

Know what to do and what NOT to do when every second counts

Emergencies

BLEEDING

    What to Do


  • Apply firm pressure with a clean cloth or bandage.
  • Keep pressing for at least 10 minutes. [don't remove the cloth if it soaks.]
  • Elevate the injured part above the heart if possible.
  • When bleeding stops, wrap with a sterile bandage.

    What Not to Do


  • Don't use a tourniquet unless bleeding is life-threatening.
  • Don't remove deeply embedded objects — apply pressure around them.
  • Don't clean large, deep wounds ,let medical help handle that.

BURN

    What to Do


  • Cool the burn under running cool water for 10 to 15 minutes.
  • Remove jewelry or tight clothing near the area (before swelling starts).
  • Cover loosely with a clean, non-fluffy cloth or sterile gauze.

    What Not to Do


  • Don't pop blisters.
  • Don't apply ice directly.
  • Don't apply butter, toothpaste, or oil.

FIRE OUTBEAK

    What to Do


  • Raise the alarm immediately
  • Get out — quickly and calmly
  • Stay low if there's smoke.
  • Close doors behind you.
  • Check doors before opening.
  • If you can't escape, stay in a room with a closed door.
  • If clothes catch fire — Stop, Drop, and Roll

    What Not to Do


  • Do not use elevators.
  • Do not open doors that are hot.
  • Do not go back in for possessions or pets.
  • Do not pour water on grease (cooking oil) fires or certain electrical fires.
  • Do not fight a large or spreading fire yourself.

POISONING

    What to Do


  • Identify what was swallowed or inhaled.
  • Call emergency services or poison control immediately.
  • If chemicals are on the skin — rinse with clean water for 20 minutes.

    What Not to Do


  • Don't make the person vomit unless told by a doctor.
  • Don't give food or drink unless advised.

CHOKING

    What to Do


  • Encourage the person to cough if they can.
  • If not breathing, perform Heimlich maneuver (abdominal thrusts).
  • For infants: 5 back blows + 5 chest thrusts.
  • If unconscious, start CPR.

    What Not to Do


  • Don't slap the person,s back if they,re coughing strongly.
  • Don't reach into their throat blindly.

SHOCK

    What to Do


  • Lay the person flat.
  • Raise legs slightly above heart level.
  • Keep them warm and calm.
  • Call for emergency help immediately.

    What Not to Do


  • Don't give anything to eat or drink.
  • Don't raise the head.

FAINTING

    What to Do


  • Lay the person flat on their back.
  • Elevate their legs slightly.
  • Loosen tight clothing around the neck.
  • When they wake, reassure and keep them resting.

    What Not to Do


  • Don't splash water on the face.
  • Don't let them get up immediately.

NOSEBLEED

    What to Do


  • Sit the person up and lean them forward slightly.
  • Pinch the soft part of the nose for 10 minutes.
  • Breathe through the mouth.
  • Apply a cold cloth over the bridge of the nose.

    What Not to Do


  • Don't tilt the head backward.
  • Don't put tissue deep inside the nose.

HEART ATTACK

    What to Do


  • Help the person sit in a comfortable position.
  • Loosen clothing.
  • Give one aspirin (if not allergic).
  • Call emergency help immediately.
  • Begin CPR if unresponsive and not breathing.

    What Not to Do


  • Don't leave the person alone.
  • Don't give food or drink

SEIZURE

    What to Do


  • Move dangerous objects away.
  • Cushion the head with something soft.
  • After jerking stops, place them in the recovery position.
  • Stay until they are fully awake.

    What Not to Do


  • Don't restrain or hold them down.
  • Don't put anything in their mouth.

ASTHMA ATTACK

    What to Do


  • Sit the person upright — do not lie down.
  • Help them use their inhaler (2 puffs every few minutes).
  • Keep them calm.
  • Call for help if no improvement.

    What Not to Do


  • Don't make them lie flat.
  • Don't leave them alone.

ELECTRIC SHOCK

    What to Do


  • Turn off the power source first.
  • Use a dry, non-conductive object (wood, plastic) to separate the person from the source.
  • Check breathing and pulse.
  • Start CPR if needed.

    What Not to Do


  • Don't touch the person while still connected to electricity.
  • Don't use metal or wet items to rescue.

DOG BITE

    What to Do


  • Wash the area with soap and water for 10 minutes.
  • Cover with sterile dressing.
  • Get medical help immediately (for rabies shot).

    What Not to Do


  • Don't ignore small bites.
  • Don't tie tightly around the wound.

CHEMICAL BURN

    What to Do


  • Rinse the affected area with clean running water for 20 minutes.
  • Remove contaminated clothing carefully.
  • Cover loosely and seek immediate help.

    What Not to Do


  • Don't apply creams or ointments.
  • Don't delay washing it off.
